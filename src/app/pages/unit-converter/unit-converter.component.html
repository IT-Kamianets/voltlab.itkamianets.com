Formula: Voltage (V) × Current (A) = Power (W)

How it works

Power is calculated by multiplying voltage and current. This basic electrical formula is commonly used to estimate device load and system requirements.

In AC systems, real power may depend on the power factor.

Energy Units

1 kilowatt-hour (kWh) equals 1000 watt-hours (Wh).

kWh is the standard unit used in electricity billing.

This conversion helps estimate battery capacity, solar production, and overall energy consumption.
